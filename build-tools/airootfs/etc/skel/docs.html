Awesome K

welcome to awesome, a tiling window manager for x11.

awesome may be a little different from what you’re used to.

If you’re coming from something like mac, windows, or other window window managers, you ought to be familiar with the concept of a window.

to you, a window can, say, overlap with another window.

they can.. cover each other, and be moved around freely.

Now, you may be perfectly happy with this behavior, in which case you can go back to GNOME, but if you’ve ever felt a bit discontent with how annoying it was to look at multiple windows at once, or wanted to have some kind of way to quickly switch to other windows, or just, you know, use the mouse less, then I have the solution for you.

So, in awesome, you have windows. We have a “tiling system”, which means when you open a window, it doesn’t appear over your current windows.

Instead, it moves your other windows out of the way, creating space for itself, so you don’t have to go back to that window because you weren’t quite done with it.

Have a try. Open your terminal, you can find it on the dock at the bottom (or use meta + enter), and see what happens to this window as you do it.

Try opening more and more terminal windows, watch as they make a big row on the right, with one large window on the left.

Now, you may be thinking, what if i wanted to distribute the space.. differently?

Press that button on the top left (shift + meta + enter), and watch the terminals move around. Isn’t that cool? Which layout is your favorite?

Alright, so you have your windows, but, inexplicably, this window has become too small to read easily.

You want to move it into the biggest position. Now, there’s two ways to do that:

  • press meta, and drag the window into the biggest position

  • press meta + shift + j to “swap with” the window to your “left”, and meta + shift + k to swap with the window to your right.

Okay, got this window back in the biggest position?

Does it feel a little too big now? You can adjust the width of this window with meta + h and meta + l respectively. You can also add another column with meta + ctrl + h/l, in certain layouts.

Alright, what do you think of awesome now?

I’ve got a new concept for you: tags.

Say your doing two things at once, but you don’t want to look at them all together. You can move some of the windows to a different tag, with shift + meta + 1-9. You can then switch to that tag with meta + 1-9. Give it a spin!

You’ve now learnt the basic concepts of tiling window managers.

From here, you can view meta + s, for the extra hotkeys.

Now, lets remove the help button from the dock.

Open up the ~/.config/awesome/src/theme/user_variables.lua (feel free to change other things besides the dock) with your favorite text editor, go over to the dock_programs line, and remove the block ({ "docs", .. }) at the end.

You can also add things to the dock, the comments explain how.

When you’re done, press meta + ctrl + r to reload awesomewm, and check out your new dock.

Feel free to check out the other files in that directory, such as ~/.config/awesome/crylia_bar/init.lua where you can change how the top bar looks.

Good luck!
